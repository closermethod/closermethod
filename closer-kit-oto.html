The 25+ Scenarios Inside

Not generic sales advice. These are real objections from real UGC creators, with word-for-word scripts that work.

What if a brand says they want to work with multiple creators to save money?
Script strategy: Acknowledge their cost concern, then reframe exclusivity as a feature (not a bug). Show them the math on audience overlap and why one great creator beats three mediocre ones. Plus, the follow-up angle to lock them in for future campaigns.
How do I respond when they say "Can you do it cheaper?"
Script strategy: Never go down in price on the first ask. Instead, use the "option menu" technique: offer 3 different tiers (reduced scope at a lower price, full scope at your rate, or premium tier with extra deliverables). They almost always pick the middle or top option.
What if they ghost after I send a proposal?
Script strategy: Don't follow up with desperation. Use the "value reminder" + timeline urgency approach. Reference a specific insight from your conversation, remind them why you were a fit, and give them a soft deadline (without being pushy). Gets replies 70%+ of the time.
How do I handle "Can you add 2 more videos for the same price?"
Script strategy: The scope creep script. Frame it as a business conversation (not a negotiation). Offer them a choice: keep the original deal or upgrade the package at a new price point. Stays friendly and preserves margin.
What if they say "We're not sure UGC is the right fit"?
Script strategy: Flip the objection. Ask diagnostic questions about their past results with other creator partnerships. Show them where UGC wins (specificity, authenticity) vs. traditional ads. Position yourself as the proof of concept.
How do I respond to "We only work with creators with X followers"?
Script strategy: Don't argue follower count. Pivot to engagement metrics and audience quality. Show them 3 examples of your best-performing content and the actual ROI data. Most brands care about results, not vanity metrics.
What if they want payment after delivery (not upfront)?
Script strategy: Agree to a split: 50% upfront to secure the dates and production, 50% on delivery. Frame it as standard industry practice (because it is). Protects you from ghosting.
How do I negotiate a retainer vs. one-off deals?
Script strategy: Show the math: monthly retainers are cheaper per video than one-offs. Offer 3 retainer tiers (2 videos/month, 4/month, 8/month) at different price points. They get predictability; you get recurring revenue.
