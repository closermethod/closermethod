Frequently Asked Questions

UGC rates in 2026 range from $150 to $1,500+ per deliverable depending on content type, niche, usage rights, and experience level. A single UGC video (30-60 seconds) typically ranges from $250-$500 for newer creators and $500-$1,200 for experienced creators. Usage rights and whitelisting add 50-200% to the base rate. These figures come from real brand deal data across enterprise, DTC, and startup categories.

In 2026, brands pay $250-$1,500+ per UGC video. A standard 30-60 second video runs $250-$500 for newer creators. Experienced creators with proven conversion data command $500-$1,200. Hook variations, b-roll packages, and multi-video bundles increase total deal value. Enterprise brands (Fortune 500, funded startups) pay 2-3x more than small DTC brands for the same deliverable.

Usage rights determine how a brand can use your content after you deliver it. Organic usage (brand posts on their feed) adds 25-50% of your base rate. Paid ad usage (whitelisting) for 30 days adds 50-100%. A 90-day paid usage license adds 100-150%. Perpetual or unlimited usage rights add 150-300%. Always negotiate usage rights separately from the creative fee. This is where most creators leave the most money on the table.

Beginner UGC creators (0-6 months) should charge $150-$350 per video in 2026. The mistake most beginners make is pricing based on follower count or experience level rather than the value the content creates for the brand. A brand running your video as a paid ad gets the same ROI whether you have 500 followers or 50,000. Start at $200-$250 per video, include basic organic usage, and charge separately for paid ad rights.

The most effective framework: 1) Never quote first if the brand reaches out to you. Ask for their budget range. 2) Separate the creative fee from usage rights. Most brands lump them together, which undervalues your work. 3) Use The Rate Anchor technique: quote 20-30% above your target so you have room to negotiate down and the brand feels like they got a deal. 4) Present rates in a professional rate card, not a DM. 5) If a brand pushes back, adjust scope (fewer revisions, shorter usage window) rather than dropping your rate.

UGC rates are based on creative output, not audience size. UGC creators make content for brands to use on the brand's channels and in paid ads. Influencer rates are based on reach and engagement on the creator's own channels. UGC rates range from $150-$1,500+ per deliverable. Influencer rates scale with follower count, from $100-$500 for nano-influencers to $10,000+ for macro-influencers. Many creators do both, but they should be priced as separate services with separate rate cards.
