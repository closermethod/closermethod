Cart Abandonment Sequences

Three independent sequences for three products. Trigger when someone visits checkout but doesn't complete the purchase. Run automatically in Kit using the cart abandon tag.

Funnel Strategy - March 2026

Purpose: Recover revenue from incomplete purchases. Each product has its own sequence with custom messaging that speaks to the objection (price, value, promise) that made them abandon.

How it works: When someone visits the checkout page for a specific product but bounces without paying, Kit applies a corresponding cart abandon tag. The automation then runs a short, focused sequence (2-3 emails) designed to remove the final objection and drive the conversion. Each sequence has its own voice and timeline - urgency increases with each email. The goal is to convert the same person who was already committed enough to reach checkout.

Kit Setup Instructions

How Cart Abandonment Tags Work

  1. Method 1: Kit Commerce Webhook - If the cart abandonment pixel is already installed on your checkout pages, the webhook automatically fires when someone leaves without completing the purchase. This applies the tag in real-time.
  2. Method 2: Manual Tag via Form - If you don't have the pixel installed, you can manually tag subscribers by adding a "cart abandonment" form or using Kit's manual tag functionality when you identify abandonment through your analytics.
  3. Method 3: Segment Import - Export your abandoned cart data from your e-commerce platform (Stripe, Gumroad, Kit Commerce) and import as a segment into Kit, then apply tags in bulk.

Creating the Three Automation Sequences in Kit

  1. Create Sequence 1: "AP Cart Abandonment"
    • Trigger: Subscriber tagged with ap-cart-abandon
    • Wait: 1 hour
    • Send Email 1: "Your playbook is waiting"
    • Wait: 24 hours
    • Send Email 2: "The math on one rate increase"
    • Wait: 24 hours
    • Send Email 3: "Last call + the downsell"
    • Exit condition: Remove if subscriber gets tagged ap-buyer, kit-buyer, or closer-buyer
  2. Create Sequence 2: "Kit Cart Abandonment"
    • Trigger: Subscriber tagged with kit-cart-abandon
    • Wait: 1 hour
    • Send Email 1: "$27 is less than one coffee run"
    • Wait: 24 hours
    • Send Email 2: "Here's what happens after you grab it"
    • Exit condition: Remove if subscriber gets tagged kit-buyer, ap-buyer, or closer-buyer
  3. Create Sequence 3: "Closer Kit Cart Abandonment"
    • Trigger: Subscriber tagged with closer-cart-abandon
    • Wait: 1 hour
    • Send Email 1: "Your upgrade is still here"
    • Wait: 24 hours
    • Send Email 2: "One retainer pays for this 10x"
    • Exit condition: Remove if subscriber gets tagged closer-buyer

Setting Up the Cart Abandonment Tag Triggers

  1. If using Kit Commerce (all purchases go through Kit.com): Kit automatically fires the cart abandonment webhook when someone lands on checkout. Configure which tag to apply for each product:
    • → Authenticity Premium checkout → apply tag ap-cart-abandon
    • → First $1K Kit checkout → apply tag kit-cart-abandon
    • → Closer Kit checkout → apply tag closer-cart-abandon
  2. If abandonment pixel is not yet installed: Go to Kit → Integrations → Commerce Webhooks. Install the pixel on your checkout pages (copy/paste the snippet into your checkout flow). The webhook will fire automatically when someone bounces.
  3. Test the tag trigger: Visit checkout yourself, leave without buying, and confirm the tag was applied to your subscriber record within 1-2 minutes.
  4. Once tags are working: The automations will fire automatically. The subscriber receives Email 1 (1 hour after abandon), Email 2 (24 hours later), Email 3 (24 hours later for AP sequence).

Post-Conversion Rules

  1. When a subscriber buys from the sequence: Kit automatically applies the purchase tag (e.g., ap-buyer). The cart abandonment sequence exits immediately - no more emails sent.
  2. If subscriber purchases a different product: For example, they abandoned the AP but bought the Kit instead. Remove them from the AP sequence, but let them keep the Kit sequence. Don't bombard with multiple sequences simultaneously.
  3. Post-purchase automation: After any purchase, wait 3-7 days, then enroll in the next upsell sequence (AP buyers → Closer Kit sequence; Kit buyers → AP upsell sequence)

Measuring Performance

  • Track cart recovery rate: % of abandoned carts that convert within 48 hours. Target: 8-12%.
  • Track email open rate: Abandon sequences typically see 40-50% open rate (higher urgency = higher opens). If below 30%, revise subject lines.
  • Track revenue recovered: Dollar value of purchases made after receiving abandon email. Set a goal to recover $500+ per week.
  • Track tag accuracy: Confirm that only the right subscribers receive the right sequence (AP abandoners get AP email, not Kit email). Audit tags monthly.

Pro Tip: Tag Overlap and Sequence Isolation

If someone abandons the AP checkout, do NOT tag them with both ap-cart-abandon AND kit-cart-abandon. One tag per abandonment. This prevents them from receiving multiple sequences at once (which is confusing and lowers conversion). If they abandon AP, then abandon Kit in the same session, update the tag to kit-cart-abandon and remove ap-cart-abandon. Last abandonment wins.

Why Cart Abandonment Works

People who reach checkout are pre-qualified buyers. They've decided to purchase - something just made them pause. A cart abandonment sequence addresses that specific pause (price, timing, final objection) rather than trying to convince someone from scratch. These sequences have 5-10x higher conversion rate than cold email sequences because the audience is already warm and committed.
