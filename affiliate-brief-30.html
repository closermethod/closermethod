The 60+ Negotiation Scripts

This is the core product. When your audience asks "what's actually in it?" - here are the answers. Click each to see the full script.

Script 1: The Rate Anchor

How to open the price conversation so YOU set the frame
▼
Thanks for reaching out about [project type] - I'd love to work together. For [number] [deliverable type] with [usage terms you want], my rate is [your rate]. That includes [list what's included: scripting, filming, editing, one round of revisions]. I structure pricing this way because it reflects the production value and strategic thinking that goes into content that actually converts - not just content that looks good. Happy to walk through what's included if helpful.

Why it works: You anchor high and justify with value, not hours. The brand negotiates down from your number instead of you negotiating up from theirs.

Script 2: The Counter-Offer

When they lowball you - give options, never say no
▼
I appreciate the offer. I'm not able to meet that rate for this scope, but here's what I can do: Option A: [reduced deliverables] at [their budget] - this removes [what you're cutting]. Option B: The full package at [your rate] - this is my standard for [deliverable type] with [usage terms]. I'd rather adjust scope than cut quality. Which works better for your team?

Why it works: Reframe the budget conversation as a scope conversation. The brand feels in control while you protect your rate.

Script 3: The Usage Rights Upsell

Turn a $500 deal into a $1,200 deal
▼
Quick question before I send the final quote - what usage are you planning for this content? If it's organic only (your brand social channels), that's included in the base rate. If you're planning paid media (Meta ads, TikTok Spark, YouTube pre-roll), I license that separately because paid distribution means the content is working harder - driving revenue, not just engagement. Paid usage: +[percentage or flat rate] for [time period]. Whitelisting: +[amount] for [time period]. I can send a quick usage rights breakdown if that's helpful.

Why it works: Most creators bundle everything and leave thousands on the table. This positions usage rights as standard industry practice.

Script 4: The Exclusivity Premium

When they want you off competing brands
▼
I can absolutely do exclusivity within [their category/niche]. My standard exclusivity premium is [amount or percentage] on top of the base rate for [time period], because exclusivity means I'm turning down other opportunities in this space during that window. For context, most brands I work with do a 30-day exclusivity window - that balances protection for your campaign with flexibility for my business. Want me to add that to the quote, or would a shorter exclusivity window work better for your budget?

Why it works: Frame exclusivity as valuable and give them a standard benchmark. The "shorter window" option lets budget-conscious brands feel like they got a deal.

Script 5: The Ghost Recovery

When they go silent after seeming interested
▼
Hey [name] - circling back on the [project type] proposal I sent on [date]. I know timelines shift on the brand side - totally understand. I'm currently booking [timeframe] projects, so I wanted to check: is this still on your radar, or has the timeline changed? If the scope or budget needs to shift, happy to revisit. If it's a no, no hard feelings - I'd rather know so I can plan my calendar. Either way, thanks for considering me.

Why it works: Creates soft urgency, gives them an easy "out" (which makes them more likely to respond), and positions you as professional, not desperate.

Script 6: The Scope Creep Defense

Prevent extra work without extra pay
▼
Just to confirm before we kick off: this project includes [number] round(s) of revisions on the final deliverables. A revision round means consolidated feedback sent in one batch - I'll turn it around within [timeframe]. If additional rounds are needed beyond that, I'm happy to accommodate at [rate per round]. I find that setting this upfront keeps the project smooth for both of us and avoids back-and-forth that delays your launch timeline. Sound good?

Why it works: Set the boundary before work begins, when you have the most leverage. Framed as their benefit - keeps things smooth, avoids delays.

Script 7: The Payment Terms

Get paid on YOUR timeline
▼
My standard payment terms are [your terms - e.g., 50% upfront, 50% on delivery] with payment due within [timeframe] of invoice. For the upfront portion: I begin production once that's received. For the balance: I deliver final files within [timeframe] of receiving the remaining payment. This structure protects both sides - you don't pay in full until you have content, and I don't deliver final assets until the project is funded. I can send an invoice via [payment method] right away. What email should I send it to?

Why it works: Present terms as standard, then immediately move to execution. The faster you move past terms to logistics, the less likely they push back.

Script 8: The Retainer Close

Turn one-off deals into recurring revenue
▼
Now that we've wrapped [project name] - the content performed [note metrics or feedback]. I wanted to float something: a lot of the brands I work with move to a monthly retainer after the first project because it locks in priority scheduling and a better per-piece rate. Here's what that looks like: [Number] pieces/month → [retainer rate] (that's [percentage]% below my per-project rate) Includes: [list deliverables, usage terms, revision rounds] Commitment: [minimum term] with [notice period] cancellation This works well for brands running always-on content - you get consistency without re-briefing a new creator every month. Want me to send a formal proposal?

Why it works: Pitch the retainer when your value is freshest - right after delivery. The "better per-piece rate" gives them financial incentive. This is how enterprise AMs turn one deal into annual contracts.
