prompts vs skills vs plugins vs mcps.

elisabeth hitz · june 18, 2026 · 7 min read

four words get thrown around like they mean the same thing: prompt, skill, plugin, mcp. they do not. and the difference is not trivia. it is the difference between collecting AI tricks and building a system that runs your work. here is the whole framework in plain english, with a table you can keep.

the fastest way to hold it: three of these are about how the AI does the work, getting more packaged as you go (prompt, then skill, then plugin). the fourth, mcp, is about access, how the AI reaches your real tools and data. instructions on one side, wiring on the other.

prompt: the instruction

a prompt is one instruction you type and retype. powerful, but it lives in your clipboard and resets every time you open a new chat. it is the rawest unit, and it is where everyone starts. a prompt is not a skill goes deeper on why the next step up matters.

skill: the reusable instruction

a skill is a folder the AI loads automatically when a task matches it. inside: a SKILL.md instruction file, and optionally reference examples, brand assets, and small scripts for the parts that must run the same way every time. you build it once and it keeps working, so quality stops depending on how well you phrased the prompt that day. more in claude skills, explained.

plugin: the bundle

a plugin packages several skills, plus the connectors and subagents they depend on, around one job. a monthly-close plugin might hold a skill for pulling the actuals, one for the variance table, one for the board memo. install it and you get the whole process the way your team does it. more in claude plugins, explained.

mcp: the access layer

mcp, the model context protocol, is the odd one out, and the one people conflate the most. it is not an instruction. it is the open standard that lets the AI plug into your real tools and data, often described as usb-c for AI: one consistent connection, and any tool that speaks it works. connectors run on mcp. it is how the AI reads your stripe, your notion, your inbox, instead of guessing. more in connect claude to your tools.

the mental model that makes it click

skills and plugins are the playbook: how the work should be done. mcp and connectors are the wiring: what the work can reach. a brilliant playbook with no wiring still guesses, because it cannot see your real data. great wiring with no playbook has access but no method. a system that actually runs your work needs both, and that is the whole point: good instructions plus real access beat a clever prompt every time.

the ladder for a small business

you do not adopt all four on day one. you climb:

  1. start with a prompt for whatever is in front of you.
  2. the prompt you keep retyping becomes a skill.
  3. a few skills that serve one workflow become a plugin.
  4. wire mcp connectors so any of it can touch your real data.
